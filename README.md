Project Overview

This project investigates the decline in revenue of a retail business despite growth in order volume. The objective was to identify the underlying drivers of revenue deterioration and provide data-driven strategic recommendations.
The analysis was conducted using Python for data cleaning and exploratory analysis, followed by interactive dashboard development in Power BI.


Business Problem


The company experienced:
* Declining revenue trends
* Increasing order volume
* Margin pressure in specific customer segments
  
 The key question addressed:
* Is the business trading revenue quality for volume growth?
  

Dataset Description


The dataset contains transactional-level retail data including:
Order ID,
Customer Segment,
Product Category,
Sales Revenue,
Quantity

Total Orders,

Revenue by Category,

Revenue vs Order Growth Trend


Key Insights

1) Revenue decline occurred despite growth in order volume, indicating deterioration in revenue quality.

2) Electronics category showed high discount levels, significantly impacting overall revenue.

3) Certain customer-category combinations generated low profitability.

4) Excessive discounting eroded margins and reduced average order value.

5) Volume growth was not translating into sustainable revenue growth.


Root Cause

The primary driver of revenue decline was excessive discounting in specific product categories and customer segments, leading to reduced revenue quality and margin erosion.


Business Recommendations

* Implement discount control thresholds for high-risk categories.

* Shift from flat discounting to bundle pricing strategies.

* Focus marketing efforts on high AOV customer segments.

* Monitor revenue quality KPIs regularly.

* Re-evaluate pricing strategy for underperforming SKUs.
